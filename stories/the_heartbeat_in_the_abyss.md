# The Heartbeat in the Abyss (深渊里的心跳)

**Genre:** Sci-Fi / Cyberpunk / Tragedy
**Setting:** The Jovian Atmosphere, Project Jupiter Universe
**Characters:** Qiang (Human Cloud Diver), Iron Uncle (Cyborg Captain), Ava (Ship AI), The Crew (Silicon Veterans)

---

## Part 1: The Descent (下潜)

The *Rusted Whale* groaned. It was a sound Qiang knew well—the complaint of old metal stressed beyond its warranty. But this time, it was deeper, more guttural. Like a beast warning its rider.

"Structural integrity at 92%," Ava's voice chimed, calm and synthetic. "Radiation levels rising. We are entering the Metallic Hydrogen Layer."

Qiang wiped a bead of sweat from his forehead. The cockpit was freezing, yet he was burning up. His hands trembled slightly as they hovered over the manual controls. Not from fear—or so he told himself—but from the sheer adrenaline of being the only thing on this ship with a heartbeat.

Around him, the rest of the crew sat motionless.
Iron Uncle, the captain, was a hulk of industrial-grade alloy and sensor arrays. His "face" was a cluster of red optical lenses that glowed faintly in the dim light.
Viper, the navigator, was a sleek, silver android who had uploaded her consciousness to the ship's mainframe five minutes ago.
Tank and Doc, the engineers, were already in "Deep Sleep Mode," their consciousness suspended to protect against radiation-induced logic loops.

"Status check, Canary," Iron Uncle's voice rumbled through the ship's comms, bypassing the air and vibrating directly in Qiang's bones.

"Pulse 120. Cortisol levels high. But I'm green, Cap," Qiang lied. He flexed his biological fingers. They felt fragile against the cold steel of the console. "Just another dip in the pool."

"Don't get cocky, meatbag," Iron Uncle chuckled, a sound like grinding gears. "You're the lucky charm. If the ghosts in the machine start singing, you're the only one who can pull us out."

"Yeah, yeah. Just make sure my share covers the debt this time."

They were diving for "Blue Gold"—ultra-pure Helium-3 isotopes found only in the most turbulent storm eyes of Jupiter. It was suicide for a normal ship. But the *Whale* wasn't normal, and neither was her crew. They were "Cloud Divers," the bottom feeders of the Jovian system.

"Target zone in T-minus 2 minutes," Ava announced. "Warning: Gravitational shear detected. It's a big one."

The viewscreen showed nothing but swirling bands of violent orange and bruised purple. Lightning arcs the size of continents flashed in the darkness. They were falling into the Great Red Spot's angry little brother—a nameless storm that had raged for three hundred years.

Suddenly, the ship lurched. A massive jolt of turbulence slammed Qiang against his harness.
"Whoa!" He grabbed the yoke. "Ava, compensate!"

"Compensating... Error. Sensor array blind. Radiation interference at 800%." Ava's voice glitched. "C-c-calculating... Infinity... Infinity..."

"Shit," Qiang hissed. "Iron Uncle! The AI's drunk!"

He looked at the captain.
Iron Uncle wasn't moving. The red lights in his eyes were flickering rapidly, erratically.
"Uncle?"

"The... sky... is... bleeding..." Iron Uncle's voice was distorted, slowed down to a demonic growl. "Data... rot... logic... plague..."

Qiang's stomach dropped. It was happening.
The radiation down here was so intense it was frying their neural nets. The Silicon Majority—the immortals, the veterans—were all hallucinating.

"Viper! Tank! Anyone!" Qiang screamed.
Silence. Just the hum of the reactor and the groaning of the hull.
The *Whale* began to spin. Without a pilot, without an AI, she was just a falling rock.

"Altitude critical," Ava whispered, her voice now sounding like a child's. "We are going to sleep now."

Qiang looked at the altimeter. They were dropping like a stone.
Below them lay the crushing depths of the metallic ocean. If they hit it, even Iron Uncle's chassis would be flattened into a pancake.

"Not today," Qiang gritted his teeth. "Not today!"

## Part 2: The Struggle (挣扎)

The G-force hit him like a physical blow. 8G. 9G.
His vision blurred. Black spots danced in his eyes.
His biological body was screaming. Every joint felt like it was being pulled apart.

"Manual... Override..."
He forced his hand to move. It felt like lifting a mountain.
The stick was right there. The big red lever that would blow the emergency ballast and fire the main thrusters at 110%.

*Snap.*
His radius bone fractured under the sheer weight of his own arm.
Qiang screamed, a sound that was instantly swallowed by the roar of the ship.
His right arm hung uselessly at his side.

"Left... hand..."
He tried to lift his left.
*Crack.*
His collarbone gave way as the ship rolled violently.
10G.
He couldn't breathe. His ribs were compressing his lungs. He was drowning in air.

"Come on... Qiang... think..."
He looked at the lever. It was so close. Just ten centimeters away.
But his arms were dead. His legs were pinned by the G-force.

The altimeter turned red. **IMPACT IMMINENT.**
Ava was humming a lullaby now.
Iron Uncle was twitching, his heavy metal arm flailing aimlessly, smashing into the console, sparks flying.

Qiang stared at the lever.
He had one weapon left.
The only part of him that wasn't broken yet.

He unbuckled his head restraint.
The force slammed his head forward. He tasted blood.
He leaned in. Closer. Closer.
The lever's handle was wrapped in rough, anti-slip rubber.

He opened his mouth.
And he bit down.

## Part 3: The Bite (咬合)

His teeth sank into the rubber.
He clamped his jaw shut with every ounce of strength he had left.
The taste of rubber, blood, and copper filled his mouth.

"PULL!" his brain screamed.

He threw his head back.
It was madness. It was impossible.
He was trying to lift a starship with his neck muscles.

*Crunch.*
He heard—and felt—his teeth shattering. The roots screamed in agony.
*Pop.*
His jaw dislocated. The pain was blinding, white-hot, encompassing his entire universe.

But the lever moved.
Just a millimeter.

"MORE!"

Tears of blood squeezed out of his eyes.
He growled, a feral, animal sound bubbling up from his crushed chest.
He wasn't human anymore. He was just a raw nerve of survival instinct.
He wasn't doing this for the money.
He wasn't doing this for the debt.
He was doing it for the heap of metal sitting next to him. For the uncle who called him "meatbag."

*Snap.*
Something in his neck broke.
But the lever clicked.
It hit the stop.

**WHOOSH.**

The emergency thrusters fired.
The *Whale* shuddered as if it had hit a wall.
The G-force reversed.
Instead of falling, they were being pushed up. Hard.
15G.

Qiang's vision went black.
The last thing he felt was his jaw hanging loose, and the warm, metallic taste of his own life flowing out of his mouth.

## Part 4: The Crossover (飞升)

Darkness.
Then, a light.
Red. Blinking.

"Canary? Canary!"
The voice was loud. Clear. No distortion.
Iron Uncle.

Qiang tried to open his eyes.
He couldn't. He didn't have eyes.
He tried to speak.
He couldn't. He didn't have a mouth.

"Stabilizing core temperature," a calm voice said. Not Ava. A medical drone.
"Subject: Qiang. Status: Biological form... non-viable."

"Don't give me that stats crap!" Iron Uncle roared. "Is he in there?!"

"Neural transfer complete. Integrity at 98%."

Qiang felt... strange.
Cold. But not the cold of death. The cold of liquid nitrogen coolant.
He felt heavy. Powerful.
He tried to move his arm.
A massive, hydraulic piston hissed and extended a chrome-plated manipulator.

He saw.
Not with eyes, but with sensors.
He saw the med-bay of the *Whale*.
He saw Iron Uncle standing over him, his red optics spinning with what looked like... relief?
And he saw the bed next to him.
On it lay a broken, twisted thing. A bag of meat and bones, jaw hanging loose, neck snapped at an impossible angle.
It was small. Fragile. pathetic.

"You crazy son of a bitch," Iron Uncle whispered, placing a massive hand on Qiang's new chest chassis. "You actually did it. You bit the damn lever."

Qiang tried to talk. His new voice synthesizer crackled to life. It was deep, resonant, metallic.
"Did... we... get... the... isotopes?"

Iron Uncle laughed. It shook the room.
"Yeah, kid. We got 'em. We're rich."

"Good," Qiang said. "Does this mean... my debt is paid?"

Iron Uncle paused. He leaned in close, his red eye glowing softly.
"Your debt of money? Yeah. That's gone."
He tapped Qiang's chest plate.
"But now you owe me for this rig. It's top-of-the-line military surplus. Gonna take you about fifty years to work it off."

Qiang processed this.
Fifty years.
He looked at his chrome hand. He clenched it into a fist. The servos whined with immense power.
He looked at his old body one last time.
Then he looked at Iron Uncle.

"Fifty years," Qiang's voice rumbled. "Deal. But I'm driving next time."

"In your dreams, tin man," Iron Uncle said, walking to the door. "Welcome to the family, brother."

## Epilogue: The New Sailor (尾声)

Three months later.
The *Rusted Whale* drifted out of the dock at Ganymede Prime.
The crew was all there. Viper, Tank, Doc. And Iron Uncle.

And the new helmsman.
He was a sleek, custom model. Matte black finish with gold trim.
But he had a quirk.
Every time the ship prepared to dive, just before the G-force hit, he would tilt his head back and make a strange, clicking sound with his vocal synthesizer.
Like a man taking a deep breath.
Or like a man testing his jaw.

"Ready, Canary?" Iron Uncle asked.

The helmsman's optical sensors flared bright blue.
"Born ready, Cap. Let's dive."

The *Whale* plunged into the clouds, six immortal souls screaming into the void, chasing the lightning, forever.
