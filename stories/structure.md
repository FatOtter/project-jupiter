
## 📝 Structure & POV: Project Jupiter (Zhulong)

### Core Narrative Concept
The story will be told through a **"Sliced" Perspective** (similar to *The Three-Body Problem*).
- **Format:** Focus on individual characters in different eras/stages of the Zhulong project.
- **Goal:** To showcase the societal evolution, technological progress, and psychological impact of this megastructure on humanity as it transitions from Type I to Type II civilization.

### Planned Phases (Brainstorming)
The story could be divided into distinct **Eras** or **Books**, each focusing on a specific phase of the Zhulong's lifecycle:

#### **Phase I: The Genesis (Idea & Early Construction)**
- **Themes:** Ambition, Sacrifice, Resource Scarcity, Political Maneuvering.
- **Key Conflict:** Why build it? Is it worth dismantling Mercury/asteroids? The sheer scale of the proposal vs. immediate needs (famine, war, climate).

#### **Phase II: The Golden Age (Operation & Stagnation)**
- **Themes:** Routine, Scientific Deadlock, Social Stratification (Inner vs. Outer System), The "Religion" of Science.
- **Key Conflict:** The accelerator has been running for generations, but no FTL breakthrough. The "Great Filter" seems insurmountable. Society begins to lose faith or repurpose the structure.

#### **Phase III: The Breakthrough (Crisis & Ascension)**
- **Themes:** Discovery, Danger, Paradigm Shift, Contact (maybe?).
- **Key Conflict:** A sudden anomaly. An experiment goes wrong (or right). The fabric of spacetime tears, or FTL is achieved but at a terrible cost.
  - **The Exodus:** The FTL drive is a success, and a significant portion of humanity departs to conquer the galaxy.

#### **Phase IV: The Singularity Trap (Post-Breakthrough & Ruin)**
- **Themes:** Unintended Consequences, Gravitational Anomalies, Survival, The "Curse" of Progress.
- **Key Concept:** The FTL (Warp) technology was achieved, but at a catastrophic cost. The massive energies used to manipulate spacetime have scarred the local fabric.
  - **The "Zero-Curvature" Trap:** The Zhulong ring itself has become a region of extreme spacetime distortion—a **"Zero-Curvature" Orbit** or a near-event-horizon singularity.
  - **The Forbidden Zone:** The Solar System is now encircled by this gravitational hazard. It is dangerous to enter or leave without advanced navigation or FTL capability.
- **Key Conflict:** Navigating a solar system now dominated by a man-made gravitational hazard. Those left behind (on Earth/Mars) are trapped within the "Eye of the Storm."

#### **Phase V: The Return (Eons Later / The Ending)**
- **Themes:** Myth, Rediscovery, Archaeology, Nostalgia, The Cycle of Civilization.
- **Key Concept:** Eons later, the descendants of the interstellar humanity (now highly advanced Type II/III civilization) return to the "Cradle" (Solar System).
  - **The Relic:** They find the Solar System quarantined or abandoned, the Zhulong still silently orbiting, its gravitational trap a monument to their ancestors' ambition.
  - **The Exploration:** A small team (perhaps using FTL to bypass the trap) enters the system to explore the ruins of Earth and the ring itself.
  - **The Irony:** They conquered the stars but lost their home. The ring that launched them also imprisoned their origin.
